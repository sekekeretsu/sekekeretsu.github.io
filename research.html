An Integrated Multi-Omics Analysis of Astrocyte–Immune Interactions in Glioblastoma

This study investigates astrocyte–immune cell interactions using an integrated multi-omics approach, combining transcriptomic, spatial transcriptomic/proteomics and spatial analyses to define cellular crosstalk within the Glioblastoma tumor microenvironment.

Characterizing MAIT cells in Glioblastoma

We used TCR sequencing, single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq), and multiplex tissue imaging with CODEX to investigate the role of mucosal-associated invariant T (MAIT) cells in glioblastoma (GBM). Our study demonstrated that MAIT cells are present within GBM tumor tissue and are associated with immunosuppressive myeloid cells. Survival analysis of the TCGA-GBM dataset showed that increased MAIT cell abundance correlates with poor patient survival. Functional studies further suggest that MAIT cells may exert an immunosuppressive role through IL-17 signaling.

Highlight of the study:

  • The expression level of MAIT signature genes correlates with the poor survival of GBM patients
  • MAIT17 cells are a predominant MAIT functional subset in GBM tumors
  • MAIT cells play immunosuppressive roles in GBM through TANs
